Coconut Copra

Coconut copra is derived from mature coconuts, after the dried and dehydrated fruit is removed from the shell. This copra has a long shelf life and finds application in the food and oil extract industry and confectionary trade. These coconuts are also used for extracting coconut oil of high graded quality.

Coir Fibre

Nachimaar Exports is a leading exporter of natural coir fibre this is extracted from matured green and brown coconut husks. It is then processed into the finest fibres which go into the making of mattresses, door mats, sacking etc.

coir fibre is of two types-brown and white. Coir obtained from fresh green coconut is termed as white fibre, which is finer than the Brown fibre. The brown fibre is the outcome of seasoned fully matured coconuts which lose their green colour in the process. Each of them has their own distinct types of applications. The brown fibre is widely used for fabrication of doormats, brushes, coir mattress and other upholstery material. Whereas the white fibre is used for making ropes, making fishing nets and other eco-friendly products.
